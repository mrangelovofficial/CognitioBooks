Taking place on the arid inhospitable desert planet Arrakis, House Atreides is tasked by the Emperor to harvest the universe's rarest resource 'spice' (Melange). Found only on Arakkis, this mystical element allows for interstellar navigation, as well as having life-prolonging capabilities when a person is exposed to it. The harvesting of this resource is not easy however, as the planet is home to a species of giant sand worms which live beneath the dunes. The planet is also home to the native Fremen tribe which worships the worms as Gods and wages war against those who come to pillage the 'spice' which they worship as part of their religion.

When Duke Leto Atreides is betrayed by House Harkonnen and murdered, his son Paul begins his jorney through the desert. After he and his mother escape during the attack, Paul is headed to join the Fremen and learn the ways of the desert in order to avenge his House and his father.

About Frank Herbert

Frank Herbert was born in 1920. He began writing in 1955 and produced one of his most famous books Dune in the period between 1959-1965. He was able to do devote himself to writing after his wife became a full-time worked in 1960 and became a full-time fiction novelist in 1972. Frank had his most commercially successful years in the 1970s to 1980s. He died in 1986 before writing his seventh novel meant to bring the Dune franchise to a definitive conclusion.
