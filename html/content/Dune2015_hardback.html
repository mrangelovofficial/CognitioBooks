Description

Dune is considered among the greatest science fiction novels of all time and was recently brought to life in a major motion picture starring Timothée Chalamet, Zendaya, Rebecca Ferguson, Oscar Isaac, Jason Momoa and more. The book is also a winner of the Hugo and Nebula awards.

The universe of Dune revolves around a rare element called 'spice' (or Melange) - the most important commodity in the unvierse, which is highly sought after by the Empreror. The drug is known to bring huge health benefits such as prolonging a person's life-span, as well as making space travel and navigation possible. The drug can only be found on a single planet called Arrakis - an arid desert and the homeworld of the Fremen - a religious tribe that has learned to survive and integrate themselves with the ways of the desert. They believe spice to be sacred and so wage war on anyone who invades their home planet and attempts to harvest it.

As the Emperor seeks to control Arrakis, and therefore control spice production, he appoints the noble House Atreides as the lords of Arrakis, a decisions which angers the former lords - House Harkonnen. The Harkonnen reclaim Arrakis by force, killing Duke Leto Atreides. This forces his son and wife Paul and Jessica to escape into the desert and seek the help of the Fremen.

For Paul to retake control of Arrakis and avenge his father, he must be accepted by the Fremen. Spice may be valuable in the world of Dune, but Arrakis hides another valuable asset. Beneath its butning sands live giant sand worms, worshipped as Gods by the Fremen, who have learned how to control them.

About Frank Herbert

Frank Herbert was born in 1920. He began writing in 1955 and produced one of his most famous books Dune in the period between 1959-1965. He was able to do devote himself to writing after his wife became a full-time worked in 1960 and became a full-time fiction novelist in 1972. Frank had his most commercially successful years in the 1970s to 1980s. He died in 1986 before writing his seventh novel meant to bring the Dune franchise to a definitive conclusion.
